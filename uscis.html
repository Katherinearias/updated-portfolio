Overview



Design Question:

How can I make the U.S Citizenship and Immigration Services website more inviting and intuitive for users seeking help with citizenship and immigration?


Solution

Conduct a heuristic evaluation of the current website and test users on its current usability. Make the website easy to read and navigate for its target users.



My Role

UI Designer

Summary

The task I was given was to redesign a government website. I chose the USCIS website because I have personally used it and have met others who have had to navigate through it. My testing supported the initial feeling I had when I first encountered the website many years ago. It is intimidating, cold, and overwhelming. I set out to make the redesign clean, direct and modern. My goal was to make it inviting and not intimidating, especially when the process of seeking U.S citizenship can be scary enough for some. I applied user research by conducting interviews, heuristic evaluations and applying information architecture. I then created a prototype for users to test after testing the original website. While my prototype still had some faults, many users found it easier to navigate through.


My Contribution

I conducted about 3 interviews for the qualitative data. I also completed our competitor analysis and picked which points to highlight that would benefit us in our design. Everyone on my team created sketches for the UI design. I then contributed to the basic colors we would use in our interface and the design of our onboarding. I, along with my teammates, conducted usability testing on our app and then pin pointed which parts in our design we could iterate on.

Process



1. I began by creating a proto-persona based on the kind of people that I knew had used the website before, which was a foreigners seeking help with naturalization (the process of becoming a U.S. citizen)



2. I then created a testing plan with the assumption that users know what they are looking for since they would be visiting the website with a purpose. I conducted a heuristic evaluation to measure the usability and pin point any issues.



3. I then asked friends and family to test out the website and point out any issues they had with it. A lot of people had similar points which included repeated links, overwhelming groups of text, and not being able to properly find what they were looking for (or not knowing when they had found it).



4. I then asked another group of people to help me card sort categories and content on the website. After evaluating all the data collected I created a site map. I also went on to create a style guide based on the colors already being used on the USCIS website to stay on brand.




5. After creating the site map and style guide I drafted the low fidelity wireframes for my redesign. Once I tested that wireframe on two users I iterated on it and added UI elements following my style guide. Finally I tested my high fidelity prototype and gathered feedback from the results. Overall, I wish I could have tested more people that fit my target user but I believe the feedback I received was extremely valuable.
