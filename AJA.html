Overview



Objective:

Transform the Atlanta Jugglers Association website to provide more to users who are not only looking to join the association, but also who are interested in learning how to juggle, hiring their services, or donating to their cause.


Solution

The navigation on the current site is cluttered and a bit confusing, as to what’s most important for our users to know. We simplified it by creating a primary navigation bar that would be most useful for users to find key features, such as how to be a part of the organization, events, about us, and how to contact.

Summary

The Atlanta Jugglers Association was in need of a website redesign. As we tested the initial website, many things that could be upgraded were pointed out. We reached out to the organization but unfortunately could not interview anyone there. We set out to make the page look more professional, but keep it fun. Our goal was to transform the Atlanta Jugglers Association website to provide more to users who are not only looking to join the association, but also who are interested in learning how to juggle, hiring their services, or donating to their cause. We felt it was valuable to help this non-profit because the Atlanta Jugglers Association does more than put on a performance. They are active members within the community aspiring to bring joy and happiness to those around them - anywhere from local parades to children’s hospital events.


My Contribution

I was heavily involved in the research phase of this project. I recommended my team and I test the original website with potential users to get some better insight. I also reached out to the organization, but unfortunately could not test our product with their members. I also added the assets to the high fidelity mobile prototype by following our style guide.

Process



1. User research was conducted collaboratively. Each team member interviewed users on their involvement within the community, and what they look for in community involvement and donation opportunities. We created our user personas based on this information.



2. The information gathered from interviews and the survey was used to create a usability testing plan for the original website. We assigned tasks for the users testing the website based on what our research indicated was important to users when seeking out a nonprofit organization to be involved in.



3. We combined information from our testing, interviews and survey to hold a brainstorming session and prioritize the features we should implement.



4. Part of brainstorming was also doing a card sorting activity. My team and I first card sorted within our group and recorded our results. We then did a card sorting session with 3 other people who gave us different insight on what users would like to see on each page outside of what we though belonged in each category, It was refreshing to have outside perspective on the website navigation. We used the card sorting data to create a sitemap.



5. We created a low fidelity prototype for mobile and desktop. We wanted to make sure to design a responsive website. We tested our lo-fi prototype on 3 people. We asked them to complete the tasks from our prior’s testing plan. We received important feedback before moving on to our high fidelity prototype. .



6. While we conducted our tests. We also worked on developing a style guide for the redesign. Our design goals were to revamp the association’s website from dated to contemporary to ignite relevance in 2020.



7. We created our high fidelity prototype, applying the guidelines we set in out style guide. We tested our prototype once more and iterated on our design based on our feedback.





*In the middle of this project, the U.S was seeing its first wave of Covid-19. This changed how our team communicated and collaborated on designs. We went from meeting 3 times a week in person to constantly communicating through Slack and meeting 3 times a week on Zoom. Although it took some adjusting, we were able to distribute the work responsibilities in order to complete the beautiful redesign that we were proud to present to our class. *
